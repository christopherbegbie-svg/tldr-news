Understanding ceasefires in global conflict

How temporary pauses in war reshape international diplomacy

  • A ceasefire is a temporary pause in fighting between conflicting nations
  • Both sides must agree for it to work—it requires trust and negotiation
  • Ceasefires create space for prisoner exchanges and humanitarian aid
  • They're not permanent peace, but critical steps toward dialogue
  • International leaders play key roles in brokering these agreements
