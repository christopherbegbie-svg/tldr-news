Maps, Power, and Elections Explained

Why redistricting shapes American democracy

  • Redistricting redraws voting district lines every 10 years after the census
  • Politicians can manipulate these boundaries to favor their party (gerrymandering)
  • Tennessee and Virginia are currently fighting over new district maps
  • These invisible lines determine representation and which policies get passed
  • Public engagement matters—citizens can challenge unfair redistricting in court
