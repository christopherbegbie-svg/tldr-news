How Empty Senate Seats Get Filled

Governors appoint replacements until voters decide permanently

  • When a senator dies in office, the state governor appoints a replacement
  • This keeps the Senate seat filled without waiting for an election
  • The appointed senator serves until a special election is held
  • Governors choose who fills the seat—a significant power
  • This system balances quick action with eventual democratic choice
