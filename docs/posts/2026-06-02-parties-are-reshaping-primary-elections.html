Parties Are Reshaping Primary Elections

Why political parties are taking charge of candidate selection

  • Primaries historically let voters freely choose party nominees
  • Many states kept parties officially neutral in these races
  • Now parties are endorsing, funding, and backing specific candidates
  • This gives party leadership more control over who runs
  • It changes the balance between grassroots voices and party power
