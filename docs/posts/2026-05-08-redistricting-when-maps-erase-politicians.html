Redistricting: When Maps Erase Politicians

How gerrymandering weakens democratic representation

  • Redistricting redraws congressional boundaries every 10 years after the census
  • When done fairly, it ensures equal representation for all voters
  • Gerrymandering happens when politicians manipulate maps to eliminate opposing districts
  • Tennessee Democrat Steve Cohen's 9th district faces potential elimination
  • This practice weakens democracy by letting politicians choose voters, not vice versa
