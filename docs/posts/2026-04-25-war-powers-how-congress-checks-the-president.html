War Powers: How Congress Checks the President

The 60-day rule that shapes military decisions and democracy

  • The War Powers Act limits presidential military authority to 60 days
  • After 60 days, Congress must vote to authorize continued war
  • This is a constitutional check on executive power
  • Approval ratings reflect public support for these decisions
  • Understanding this system makes you a more informed citizen
