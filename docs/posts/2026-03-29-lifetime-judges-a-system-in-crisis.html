Lifetime judges: A system in crisis?

Why a 98-year-old judge's case challenges American law

  • Pauline Newman is 98 and fighting to keep her federal judgeship
  • U.S. federal judges have lifetime tenure—they serve until they choose to retire
  • America's judiciary is aging: some judges are in their 90s and still working
  • Lifetime tenure was designed to protect judicial independence, but it creates delays and bottlenecks
  • This case forces America to ask: Should there be age limits or mandatory retirement for judges?
