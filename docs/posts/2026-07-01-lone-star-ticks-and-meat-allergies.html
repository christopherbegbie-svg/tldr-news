Lone Star Ticks and Meat Allergies

How a tiny tick is changing American diets

  • Lone star ticks are spreading rapidly across the U.S.
  • They carry a molecule called alpha-gal that triggers immune responses
  • One bite can develop into a red meat allergy
  • Affects beef, pork, lamb, and dairy products
  • Thousands of Americans now have alpha-gal syndrome
