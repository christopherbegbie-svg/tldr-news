The Meteoric Rise and Fall of Nokia

From making 1 in 3 phones to selling out in 9 years

  • In 2005, Nokia sold its billionth phone to a customer in Nigeria
  • At its peak, Nokia made 1 out of every 3 phones globally
  • The Finnish company rose from obscurity in the 1990s to worldwide phenomenon
  • By 2014—just 9 years later—Nokia sold its entire handset division to Microsoft
  • The sale price was a fraction of what the division was worth at its peak
