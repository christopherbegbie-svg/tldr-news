Can Elections Be Cancelled Legally?

Mexico's new law raises democracy questions worldwide

  • Mexico's senate passed a constitutional amendment to annul elections for 'foreign interference'
  • The law defines interference as illegal financing, propaganda, and disinformation
  • Critics fear it gives the ruling party power to overturn any election results
  • The amendment's vague language could be misused by whoever's in power
  • This raises urgent questions about how democracies protect themselves fairly
