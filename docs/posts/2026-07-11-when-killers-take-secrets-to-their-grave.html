When Killers Take Secrets to Their Grave

The Falconio case shows why closure matters in criminal justice

  • Peter Falconio, a 28-year-old UK backpacker, was killed in the Australian outback in 2001
  • Bradley Murdoch was convicted of the murder but never revealed where the body was hidden
  • 25 years later, police released body-camera footage showing Murdoch's aggressive denial
  • Murdoch died without sharing the location, leaving the family without closure
  • This case highlights how criminal justice seeks both punishment and answers for victims' families
